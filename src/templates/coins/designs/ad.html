{{ _("What even is a chamois?") }}

{{ _("1-, 2-, and 5 Euro Cent Coins") }}

{% trans %} These coins prominently feature a Pyrenean chamois in the foreground with a bearded vulture flying above. Both animals are native to Andorra. {% endtrans %}

{{ _("10-, 20-, and 50 Euro Cent Coins") }}

{% trans %} These coins feature the Church of Santa Coloma d’Andorra, the oldest church in Andorra. The church is dedicated to Columba of Sens, the patron saint of Andorra and dates back to the 8th century. {% endtrans %}

{{ _("1 Euro Coins") }}

{% trans %} This coin features the Casa de la Vall, the former headquarters of the General Council of Andorra. It was built in 1580 as a manor and tower defense by the Busquets family. In 1702, it was acquired by the Consell de la Terra. {% endtrans %}

{{ _("2 Euro Coins") }}

{% trans %} This coin features the Andorran coat of arms. The coat of arms features the four different shields of the Bishop of Urgell, the Count of Foix, Catalonia, and the Viscounts of Béarn. At the bottom is the motto ‘Virtus Unita Fortior’ which translates to ‘Virtue United is Stronger’. {% endtrans %}
